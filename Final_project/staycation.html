Facts about Timp ▼

Mount Timpanogos, often referred to as "Timp," is estimated to be over 300 million years old, with its unique limestone formations dating back to ancient seabeds. The Timpanogos Cave system, discovered in the early 20th century, features rare helictites—formations that grow in twisting, gravity-defying shapes. A fun fact about the mountain is that it is steeped in local folklore, including a legend about a Native American princess whose tears are said to have formed Emerald Lake. The mountain is also home to one of the longest-running annual hikes in the United States, attracting thousands of visitors each year. With its rich history, geological wonders, and cultural significance, Timpanogos continues to captivate adventurers and nature enthusiasts alike.

Timpanogos Cave National Monument

Mount Timpanogos is a stunning mountain located in the Wasatch Range of Utah, known for its breathtaking views, wildflower-covered meadows, and challenging hiking trails. It’s a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts who want to experience the beauty of nature and the thrill of reaching a summit over 11,000 feet high. Whether you’re hiking to Emerald Lake or exploring the scenic alpine terrain, Mount Timpanogos offers unforgettable adventures for visitors of all ages.

Reasons Why You Should Go

  • Experience breathtaking panoramic views from the summit.
  • Explore the stunning Timpanogos Cave National Monument.
  • Enjoy vibrant wildflower meadows during the summer months.
  • Challenge yourself with scenic and adventurous hiking trails.
